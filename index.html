Amusement Park Ticket

INTRODUCTION

 The Amusement Park dataset comprises of tickets purchase details of registered and unregistered customers in Wonderland. It includes 6 variables namely: Time, Temperature, Humidity, Wind speed, Ticket 1 and Ticket 2; wherein are sold tickets and response variables. Eight machine learning algorithms: Linear, Ridge, Lasso Regression, Principal Component Analysis, Generalized Additive Model, Rgression Decision Tree, Random Forest and Boosting for decision tree have been implemented in model selection. The packages 'glmnet','pls','gam','rpart','randomForest' & 'gbm' were used in analysis.

DATA PROCESSING

 The variable 'Time' was seperated into 7 new variables: Year, Month, Day, Weekday, Hour, Minute, and Second. After considering varaibles' level and correlations, only Year, Month, Weekday and Hour were selected and treated as categorical variables for futher analysis. For numerical variables: Temperature, Humidity and Wind speed, the correlation rates were low, so there was no interaction term considered to fit in models, and z-score normalization technique was used to adjust these three numeric variables. 10-fold cross validation was implemented for model selection.

CONCLUSION

 By comparing MSE (Mean Square Error) values in Ticket 1 and Ticket 2 models, variables 'Year','Month','Weekday','Hour','Temperature','Humidity' and 'Windspeed' were used to predict number of Ticket 1 and Ticket 2. The Boosting method (MSE=229.87 & 1573.59)and Random Forest(MSE=228.48 & 1523.96) had similar MSE. However, Random Forest was much more slower than Boosting. Therefore, to predict Ticket 1, the Boosting with iteration 1000, the maximum depth of each tree was 7 and learning rate was 0.1. To predict Ticket 2, the Boosting with iteration 1000, maximum depth of each tree was 6, learning rate was 0.1.
